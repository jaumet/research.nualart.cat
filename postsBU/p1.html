Crossreads III is a exploration tool for the  collection “Word War I Diaries” from the State Library of New South Wales (Australia). This site is an interface to that collection developed as part of a research project called “Crossreads” by Jaume Nualart (PhD Candidate at University of Canberra, Faculty of Arts and Design. 2015).

This project proposes a way of exploring and reading the collection based on grouping pages according to the topics they talk about. The analysis has been automated with topic models. We are preparing a publication with a detail description about how this tool was created and its novelty aspects.

Overview of the collection:

Basic metadata of the collection:

  • 49 authors: all Anzac, aussies soldiers
  • 126 Diaries: from 2 pages long, to 319 pages long
  • 11944 pages: crowd-transcribed to text, by users of SLNSW
  • 100 years ago: dated from 1914, to 1920

Topics are presented in 5 groups:

  • Personal: , Weather, Colors of life, Food, Personal care & clothes, Letters & family, Poetry & spirit, Family & Oz, In French
  • War: Combat, Front line, Health, Air, Context
  • Military life: General, Politics & context, Parades & Marches, Turkey, •Sports
  • Travelling: Roads & railways, Over the sea, Middle East
  • Tha accidental tourist: City life, Egypt, UK, France
